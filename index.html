Planet Earth

The Birth of the Planet


Earth was born about 4.5 billion years ago when dust and gas left over from the formation of the Sun collided and stuck together under gravity, forming a molten young planet that later cooled, developed a crust, released gases to create an atmosphere, and eventually formed oceans where life could begin.

Active Recycling

Earth's rock cycle, driven by tectonic plate movements, constantly recycles material, transforming igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks over time.

Four key facts about Earth

Earth is the only planet in the solar system known to harbor life.

Water covers roughly 70% of the Earth's surface, making it unique in having water in all three forms: solid, liquid, and gas.

Earth has a solid, rocky surface with a "squishy" interior, featuring a hot inner core of iron and nickel that is roughly as hot as the surface of the sun.

The atmosphere protects the planet from meteors and solar radiation, providing the perfect balance for life.
